System of information sharing

There is various indigenous knowledge which used by the local people to manage and control floods and droughts. These measures are used as a substitute of modern tools like boats for evacuating people during floods from precarious to safer places. In practice, the effectiveness of these techniques depended on the flexibility and fastness of the people.

Gender, age, and body fitness of a person are among the key factors for effective running, swimming, and climbing trees and roofs of the houses. Furthermore, it is depicted that men are flexible and fast to run, swim and climb trees and roofs of the houses compared to women, especially those who are under maternity. In addition, it is revealed that women are often restricted from running fast by their clothing, their role as caretakers of children and older people as well as cultural rules restrict them from leaving their homes without the accompaniment of a male relative. Nonetheless, children and elders are perceived to be lagging behind to run, swim, and climb trees and roofs of the houses due to age and body fitness factors.

Also, people are planting indigenous vegetation to curb the intensity of floods and people are using traditional trumpets (cow horns) and whistles to liaise information about flood incidents. Where by once people hear sirens from cow horns and whistles they become sentient to run away from precarious places to safe places. furthermore, ascertain that there are specific voices from those traditional tools which distinguish the occurrence of floods and other menaces. Local people views on the use of local instruments to communicate information about flood concurs that traditional media and tools are instrumental in addressing disasters insofar as they are easily affordable and accessible in liaising disaster warnings and information.

  • Swimming

  • Climbing trees/roofs

  • Crying

  • Running

  • Hiding in/on safer places

  • Storing food, water

  • Planting of native vegetation (reeds and bamboos)

  • Construction of embankment

  • Contouring and terracing
