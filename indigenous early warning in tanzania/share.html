System of information sharing

Various scenario entail as to why we need warnings. These may be but not limited to the following;

  • To achieve safety, warnings have been part of our daily life.
  • We live because we need security of our life, as well as our assurance of reaching development. In such a view, we need timely and accurate warnings daily.
  • To enhance preparedness in all settings (physical, social, economic, psychological , biological, technological, etc.).
  • To reduce loss of lives.
  • To reduce injuries.
  • To reduce costs of response and recovery measures, etc.

Therefore, Various tools can be used for communication & dissemination of EW information. having the potential to benefit end users and save lives.

  • Street lauding (Sukuma, zinza, kurya, zanaki, sumbwa, massai, Kaguru, Sagala and Vidunda. Once flood or drought happens people or society get information through street lauding that is made by special people chosen in the community to raise awareness of people and to share information about the occurrence of either flood or drought.
  • Meetings (used by sukuma, zinza,kurya, zanaki, sumbwa, massai Kaguru, Sagala and Vidunda Blowing traditional trumpets (cow horns) and whistles (used by Sukuma and massai community). After the early signs show that something bad (i.e. flood and drought) will happen people gather at a big tree and have a meeting to discuss and share information to people of what has been foreseen so as to enhance coping strategies and response measures against disasters.
  • Drums, bells (used by Sukuma,sumbwa and zinza tribes).also in other community they use to beat drums and ring bell so as to share information to people about what is going to happen in order to enhance response measures such as food security, coping mechanisms and others.
