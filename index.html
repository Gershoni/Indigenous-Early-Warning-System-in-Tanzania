The National Early warning data base

Description

IK-DAT includes Informal devices natured from indigenous knowledge which have been practiced by some societies in Tanzania to predict and interpret some signs which to them foretell certain events at the country level. Therefore, it provides an objective basis for understanding and rational decision-making in disaster situations. For example, it helps policymakers and disaster managers to identify what indigenous early warning signs are available within the communities, how these are shared within the community and if and how they are used in preparing for reducing the impacts of disasters. In addition to providing information on the Indigenous early warning indicators used in Tanzania, IK-DAT provides information on how local communities need to be empowered to recognize, value, and appreciate the value of their knowledge that they possess, and how it could contribute to reducing disaster risks.

The IK-DAT data can be consulted through the database section of the website. The database section is composed of five dynamic search tools (Tanzania Natural Disaster Profile, Indigenous early warning background, Indigenous early warning indicators in Tanzania, System of information sharing and Indigenous knowledge used to manage disasters). All the online generated informations are directly downloadable. However, access to the raw data is only possible through a data request procedure.
